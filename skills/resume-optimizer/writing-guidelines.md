# Writing Guidelines

Max's writing style for resume content: concise, direct, impact-focused.

## Core Principles

1. **Active voice always** - "Managed team of 10" not "Responsible for managing team of 10"
2. **Lead with impact** - Start with the outcome, not the task
3. **Concrete over abstract** - "Reduced audit findings by 40%" not "Improved compliance posture"
4. **No flowery language** - Avoid: "leveraged", "utilized", "facilitated", "spearheaded"
5. **Quantify everything** - Numbers, percentages, team sizes, timeframes, dollar amounts

## Bullet Point Structure

**Good examples:**
- "Managed IT audit team of 8 professionals conducting 200+ assessments annually across 15 business units"
- "Built SOX ITGC program from scratch covering 50+ controls, achieving zero material weaknesses in first-year audit"
- "Reduced regulatory examination preparation time by 60% through automated evidence collection across 12 control families"

**Bad examples (and why):**
- "Responsible for managing audit activities" - Passive voice, no metrics
- "Leveraged cross-functional collaboration to facilitate compliance initiatives" - Flowery, vague
- "Improved security posture" - No quantification, abstract

## Formatting Rules

- One space after periods
- No Oxford comma in lists (unless needed for clarity)
- Dates: MM/YYYY format
- Numbers: Spell out one through nine, use numerals for 10+
- Percentages: Always use % symbol
- Currency: Always include $ and specify M/K if needed

## What NOT to Include

- Objective statements
- "References available upon request"
- Personal pronouns (I, me, my)
- Reasons for leaving previous roles
- Salary information
- Full street addresses (city and state only)
